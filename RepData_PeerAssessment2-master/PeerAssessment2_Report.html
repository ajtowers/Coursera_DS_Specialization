US Most Harmful Weather-Related Events since 1950

Synopsis

The present report answers the questions of which weather-related events in the United States have had the largest impact measured as fatalities and injuries in populations, as well as the economic losses in property and crops.
The analysis was performed using the info recorded in the U.S. National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administrations (NOAA) storm database that stores quantitative and qualitative data since 1950.
Tornados are the main cause of fatalities and injuries, and the third in economic losses during the period covered by the dataset.
Floods are the main cause of economic losses in property and crops.

And finally load the definition of a function used to convert data about economic losses into million of dollars.

# Function to convert in economic units
to_DollarAmount <- function(amount, unit = ''){
    # Convert 'amount' dollars depending on the value of 'unit'
    # If 'unit' is not in [K,k,M,m,B,b] assumes that 'amount' is in dollars
    x <- ifelse(unit == 'K' | unit == 'k', amount * 1000,
                ifelse(unit == "M" | unit == 'm', amount * 1000000,
                       ifelse(unit == "B" | unit == 'b', amount * 1000000000,
                              amount
                              )
                       )
                )
    # Return transformed to millions
    return(x/1000000)
}

Build the second working datatable, dtw_2, to calculate and store economic damages measured in million dollars.

The function to_DollarAmount is used to transform units (K, M and B) into numeric values to convert figures to million dollars.
Two new datatables are created:

  • top_events_dmg store the list of the 10 most harmful Type of Events ordered by the economic damage in million dollars in Property and Crops.

  • top_states_dmg store the list of the 3 most affected States ordered by dollar amount of damages to Property and Crops.

# Select rows with property and/or crop economic damages > 0 
# Select and rename columns to retrieve, and applies function to convert and 
# calculate dollar amounts in millions.
dtw_2 <- dt[PROPDMG > 0 | CROPDMG > 0,
            list(Event = EVTYPE, State = STATE,
                 PropDmgT = to_DollarAmount(PROPDMG, PROPDMGEXP), 
                 CropDmgT = to_DollarAmount(CROPDMG, CROPDMGEXP)
                 )
            ]
# Accumulate total amount of damages in million dollars by event, store in 
# TotDmg variable and order by it.
dtw_2 <- dtw_2[ , TotDmg := (PropDmgT + CropDmgT)][order(-TotDmg)]

# Eliminates unused columns
dtw_2 <- dtw_2[ , c('PropDmgT', 'CropDmgT') := NULL]

# Accumulates total amount of damages by State and Event
dtw_2 <- dtw_2[ , lapply(.SD, sum), by = c('State', 'Event')]

# Top 10 list of Type of Event ordered by amount of economic damage

Results

1. Across the United States, which types of events (as indicated in the EVTYPE variable) are most harmful with respect to population health?

a. Most harmful Events based on Fatalities

The following graph includes the list of the most harmful Type of Events over the period recorded in the dataset, ordered by the number of direct Fatalities caused.

The most harmful type of event measured as the number of fatalities over the period sampled, is the TORNADO event. It caused 5633 direct fatalities.

The US State with the largest amount of recorded deaths due to this type of events is AL with 617 fatalities, representing a 4.1% of total number of deaths.

b. Most harmful Events based on Injuries

The next plot includes the list of the most harmful Type of Events over the period recorded in the dataset, ordered by the number of direct Injuries caused.

Taken the number of injured people, the most harmful type of event in the dataset, is the TORNADO event, that caused 91346 direct Injuries.

The US State with the largest amount of injured people is TX that suffered 8207 injuries, accounting for 5.8% of the total.
